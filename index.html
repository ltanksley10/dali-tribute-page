Salvador Dali

A tribute to the master of surrealism..

His life and artwork from 1904-1989:

  • May 11, 1904 - Salvador Domingo Felipe Jacinto Dalí i Domènech is born in Figueres, Catalonia - the son of a middle-class lawyer.
  • 1916 - Dali began attending a drawing school in Figueres. That summer he also went on a trip to Cadaques where his love for painting began.
  • 1918 - His first public exhibition was organized at the Municipal Theatre in Figueres - a collection of his best charcoal drawings.
  • 1922 - The year he moved to Madrid to study at the San Fernando Royal Academy of Fine Arts. During his time in Madrid he would attend the Prado Musem every Sunday to study the great works of art they showcased.
  • 1926 - Dali meets Pablo Picasso in Paris - an artist who influenced him greatly throughout his life.
  • 1927 - Surrealism begins taking a hold of Dali and this shows more and more in his painting style, such as in his paintings 'Honey is Sweeter than Blood' and 'Apparatus and Hand.'
  • 1929 - He meets his future wife, Elena Ivanovna Diakonova, better known as Gala. She became his muse for life. Dali's father disapproved of their romance and would soon expel him from their family home.
  • 1931 - The year he creates one of his most famous works of art - 'The Persistence of Memory.'
  • 1934 - Salvador and Gala are married in Paris. That same year Dali also travelled to America for the first time.
  • 1936 - The year he was pictured on the cover of Time Magazine.
  • 1938 - He's introduced to Dr. Sigmund Freud, who he sketched a portrait of during their visits
  • 1939 - World War II begins while the Dali's are in France. At some point after the invasion they were able to escape and cross into Portugal, where they then travelled to America.
  • 1941 - During an exhibition in New York in which he speaks about returning to form, control, and structure and claims the Surrealist movement is dead. This exhibition showcased his paintings 'Slave Market with the Disappearing Bust of Voltaire' and 'The Face of War.'
  • 1943 - An autobiography of Dali is published titled, The Secret Life of Salvador Dali.
  • 1943 - He continues his attack on the Surrealist Movement stating, "Surrealism will at least have served to give experimental proof that total sterility and attemps at automizations have gone too far and have led to a totalitarian system.."
  • 1945 - Dali showcased more of his new classicism style at another exhibition in Neq York. This included paintings, 'Basket of Bread' and 'My Wife Nude Contemplating Her Own Flesh becoming stairs, Three Vertebrae of a Column, Sky and Architecture.'
  • 1946 - The year he worked with Walt Disney to produce the animated film, Destino.
  • 1948 - Dali holds another exhibition which highlights his interest in atomic physics. This exhibition showcased paintings such as 'Dematerialization Near the Nose of Nero' and 'Intra-Atomic Equilibrium of a Swan's Feather.' Early that year he also published a book, Dali's 50 Secrets of Magic Craftsmanship.
  • 1950 - His fathe dies and Dali finds out he has been disinherited from his will due to a falling out they had over his marriage to Gala.
  • 1954 - During the mid 1950s Dali was fascinated by shapes such as the rhinoceros horn, which he believed signified divine geometry as it grows in a logarithmic spiral, and the tesseract(a four-dimensional cube). This was shown in his painting, 'Crucifixion(Corpus Hypercubus).'
  • 1960 - Dali began working on The Dali Theatre and Museum in his town of Figueres. Additions were made to the museum up until the mid 1980s.
  • 1965 - After being introduced to the fashion model, Amanda Lear, he falls in love and they are united in a 'spiritual marriage.'
  • 1969 - 'The Hallucinogenic Toreador' is completed, inspired by his wifes dislike for bullfighting.
  • 1980 - Dali's health begins to decline quickly, and he was said to be treated for depression, drug addiction and Parkinson symptoms.
  • 1982 - Gala died on June 10th at the age of 87, and Salvador moved to her castle where she was entombed.
  • 1983 - The year that marked the last official painting by Dali, 'The Swallow's Tail.' It's said that, 'this painting was heavily influenced by the mathematical catastrophe theory of Rene Thom.'
  • 1984 - His depression became more severe causing him to refuse food at times. In August of that year a fire was started by an electrical fault in his room leading to Dali being hospitalized due to burns from the fire.
  • 1986 - In July Dali was fitted with a pacemaker on his heart, and when he returned to his Theatre-Museum he stated, " When you are a genius, you do not have the right to die, because we are necessary for the progress of humanity."
  • 1988 - In November he enters a hospital with heart failure. In December he is visited by King Juan Carlos, who admits he was a great fan of Dali. Dali gifts him with a drawing, 'Head of Europa,' which is now known as his final drawing.
  • 1989 - On January 23rd Dali passed away from heart failure while listening to his favorite record of Tristan and Isolde. He is buried in a crypt below the stage of his Theatre-Museum in Figueres, Catalonia, Spain.
